Heart Disease Classification Prediction

For the machine learning piece, we used a sample Heart Disease dataset from Cleveland, OH. The dataset has 14 factors in total with some being age, sex, maximum heart rate achieved, serum cholesterol, etc. We have built a regression and concluded with a classification prediction. We are predicting if a person with these factors will have heart disease, so our Y was a binary classification “Target” of (0- No Heart Disease or 1- Heart Disease).

We created 4 different models: K Neighbors Classifier, Neural Network, Random Forest and Support Vector Machine. The Random Forest model was best-fit for our training and test data split. Compared to the other models, this was our best-fit because it had a higher Training Data Score: 1.0 and Testing Data Score: 0.6447. In addition, the F1-score was 0.82. The top 4 relevant factors of classifying a person with heart disease are age, maximum heart rate achieved, serum cholesterol, and ST depression induced by exercise relative to rest.
